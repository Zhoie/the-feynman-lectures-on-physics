# Momentum Conserved with Real Collision Loss Channels (SI)

Collision restitution is velocity-dependent `e(v_rel)`. A separate
non-conservative loss term models rotational/thermal sinks. Energy is evaluated
in pre/post windows around the first collision.

## What you can do
- Change **masses**, **initial velocities**, and **base restitution**.
- In **Advanced**, tune restitution speed-slope and non-conservative loss.

## What to notice
- Momentum remains the primary conserved quantity in isolated mode.
- `Kpost/Kpre` is measured from a collision window, not a trailing average.
- The calibration chart compares simulation against benchmark energy-loss data.
